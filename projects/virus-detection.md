This senior project applied Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) to detect the presence of viruses (such as COVID-19) or other substances in a liquid test subject. As a proof of concept, we measured sugar concentration in liquid samples and achieved approximately 88% detection accuracy.

My contributions spanned both hardware and software: I designed and 3D-printed electrode models using SolidWorks, and designed and simulated a Potentiostat circuit model using the Falstad circuit simulator. The project bridged electrical engineering and biomedical sensing, requiring me to apply skills across mechanical design, circuit analysis, and embedded systems.
